# Proposal: PhD Travel / Visiting Scholarship in Statistics

## 1. Overview

This proposal outlines the establishment of a PhD Travel / Visiting Scholarship within the Department of Statistics at the University of Auckland. The purpose of this scholarship is to support high-quality research collaboration through structured academic visits, either by:

- PhD students from overseas institutions visiting the University of Auckland, or  
- PhD students in Statistics at the University of Auckland undertaking a research visit at an external institution  

The scholarship is intended to strengthen existing research collaborations, foster wider collaboration networks, and produce high-quality research outputs.

The scheme recognises that benefits may extend beyond academia, including to industry, government, and other applied research settings.

---

## 2. Funding

- Value: Up to NZD $10,000 per annum  
- The scholarship may be awarded to one or more recipients per year, at the discretion of the selection committee  

The scholarship is funded through:

- The Wickham Family donation to the University of Auckland Data Science programme (primary source), and  
- A contribution from the Department of Statistics scholarships fund  

This funding structure is intended to:

- Support PhD student research and mobility in Statistics and Data Science  
- Provide a structured and strategic use of existing student-focused funds  
- Avoid material impact on other research funding streams (e.g., staff travel funding)  

To ensure flexibility and sustainability:

- The scholarship may be awarded as full or partial support  
- Co-funding from other sources (e.g., research grants, institutional support, or host contributions) is encouraged where appropriate  

The scholarship may be named to recognise the contribution of the Wickham Family donation.

---

## 3. Eligibility Criteria

### 3.1 General Requirements

Applicants must:

- Be currently enrolled in a PhD programme in Statistics, or a closely related discipline with a clear statistical or data science component  
- Be at least in their second year of study (or equivalent)  
- Demonstrate strong academic performance and research capability  
- Be up to date with all required doctoral milestones and progress requirements at their home institution  

### 3.2 University of Auckland Students

Applicants who are PhD students in Statistics at the University of Auckland must:

- Be supervised within the Department of Statistics  
- Have successfully passed their provisional year review (confirmation of candidature)  
- Be up to date with all required doctoral milestones and departmental progress requirements  
- Have a supervisor (or relevant Department of Statistics academic) who has a demonstrable, pre-existing academic relationship with a researcher at the proposed host institution  

### 3.3 Visiting Students to New Zealand

Applicants from overseas institutions must:

- Be enrolled in a PhD programme at a recognised institution  
- Have a supervisor who has a demonstrable, pre-existing academic relationship with a member of academic staff in the Department of Statistics at the University of Auckland  

### 3.4 Other New Zealand-Based Students

Applicants who are enrolled in a PhD programme at another New Zealand institution must:

- Be enrolled in a PhD programme in Statistics, or a closely related discipline with a clear statistical or data science component  
- Be at least in their second year of study (or equivalent)  
- Be up to date with all required doctoral milestones and progress requirements at their home institution  
- Have a supervisor who has a demonstrable, pre-existing academic relationship with a member of academic staff in the Department of Statistics at the University of Auckland  

Priority will be given to applications that build on clearly demonstrated existing collaborations.

---

## 4. Nature of the Visit

- Visits may be inbound (to the University of Auckland) or outbound (from the University of Auckland)  
- The duration of the visit must be no less than six weeks  
- The duration of the visit should be appropriate to the proposed research project (typically between 6 weeks and 6 months)  
- The visit must be hosted by an academic within the relevant department at the destination institution  
- The visit must clearly benefit the PhD student and, where applicable, the University of Auckland academic involved  

---

## 5. Collaboration Requirement

A key principle of this scholarship is to support and deepen existing academic collaborations.

- Applications must clearly document the prior relationship between the relevant academic staff (e.g., joint publications, prior visits, grants, or ongoing research projects)  
- For University of Auckland applicants, there must be a demonstrable, pre-existing relationship between a Department of Statistics academic and a researcher at the host institution  
- For visiting students, there must be a demonstrable, pre-existing relationship between the applicant’s supervisor and a Department of Statistics academic  

Evidence may include joint publications, prior visits, shared grants, or other sustained research interaction.

---

## 6. Research Project

Each application must include a clearly defined research project to be undertaken during the visit.

The proposal should:

- Outline the research objectives and methodology  
- Demonstrate how the visit will contribute to the project’s success  
- Include a feasible timeline for completion  
- Identify expected research outputs  

The following conditions apply:

- Projects focused solely on the completion or writing up of a PhD thesis are not eligible  

Preference will be given to projects where:

- A primary goal is the preparation and submission of a peer-reviewed publication within a reasonable timeframe  
- High-quality outputs may also include substantive technical reports or other scholarly contributions where appropriate  
- The collaboration meaningfully advances the student’s PhD research  

---

## 7. Application Requirements

Applicants must submit:

- A research proposal (maximum 2–3 pages)  
- A statement of purpose outlining the benefits of the visit  
- Evidence of the existing collaboration between supervisors/hosts  
- A brief CV of the student  
- Letters of support from:  
  - The student’s primary supervisor  
  - The host academic at the destination institution  

---

## 8. Selection Criteria

Applications will be assessed based on:

- Academic merit of the applicant (e.g., publications, progress reports, referee assessments)  
- Quality and feasibility of the proposed research project  
- Strength and evidence of the existing collaboration  
- Likelihood of producing high-quality research outputs (e.g., publications)  
- Strategic value to the Department of Statistics at the University of Auckland  
- Alignment with Data Science and Statistics priorities, reflecting the Wickham Family funding  

In addition:

- Consideration will be given to equity and opportunity, including the extent to which the applicant has had access to similar opportunities or funding  
- Consideration will be given to the distribution of awards across supervisors and research groups to avoid concentration within a small number of groups  

Assessment will be holistic, taking into account disciplinary norms.

---

## 9. Administration

- The scholarship will be administered by the Head of Department of Statistics at the University of Auckland, or their delegate (most likely the Deputy Head (Research))  
- A selection committee comprising academic staff will review applications  

Recipients will be required to:

- Deliver a full departmental seminar (approximately 50 minutes) following completion of the visit  
- Provide a brief written report on the outcomes of the visit  

---

## 10. Expected Outcomes

The scholarship is expected to:

- Foster wider and sustained research collaboration networks  
- Enhance the research experience and output of PhD students  
- Increase the rate of high-quality joint publications  
- Raise the research profile of the Department of Statistics at the University of Auckland  

Where feasible, outcomes will be monitored through indicators such as joint publications, ongoing collaborations, and subsequent research activity.
