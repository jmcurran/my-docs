# Proposal: NZSA PhD Travel Scholarship (Outbound) in Statistics and Data Science

## 1. Overview

This proposal outlines the establishment of a PhD Travel Scholarship sponsored by the New Zealand Statistical Association (NZSA). The purpose of this scholarship is to support high-quality research collaboration through structured academic visits by New Zealand-based PhD students to external institutions (within New Zealand or internationally).

The scholarship is intended to strengthen existing research collaborations, foster wider collaboration networks across the New Zealand statistical community, and produce tangible research outputs.

## 2. Funding

- Value: NZD $10,000 per annum  
- The scholarship may be awarded to one or more recipients per year, at the discretion of the selection committee  
- Funds are intended to support travel, accommodation, and reasonable research-related expenses  

## 3. Eligibility Criteria

### 3.1 General Requirements

Applicants must:

- Be currently enrolled in a PhD programme in Statistics, Data Science, or a closely aligned quantitative discipline  
- Be at least in their second year of study (or equivalent)  
- Demonstrate strong academic performance and research capability  

### 3.2 New Zealand-Based Students

Applicants must:

- Be enrolled in a PhD programme at a New Zealand university or a polytechnic with the authority to award PhDs  
- (This includes applicants who are employees of a Public Research Organisation (PRO) but are formally enrolled in a PhD at such an institution)  
- Have successfully passed their provisional year review (or equivalent milestone)  
- Have a supervisor (or relevant academic) who has a demonstrable, pre-existing academic relationship with a researcher at the proposed host institution  

## 4. Nature of the Visit

- Visits are outbound from a New Zealand institution to an external host institution (within New Zealand or internationally)  
- The duration of the visit must be no less than six weeks  
- The duration of the visit should be appropriate to the proposed research project (typically between 6 weeks and 6 months)  
- The visit must be hosted by an academic or researcher at the destination institution  
- The visit must clearly benefit the PhD student and, where applicable, the New Zealand-based academic or research partner  

## 5. Collaboration Requirement

A key principle of this scholarship is to support and deepen *existing* academic collaborations.

- Applications must clearly document the prior relationship between the relevant academic staff (e.g., joint publications, prior visits, grants, or ongoing research projects)  
- There must be a demonstrable, pre-existing relationship between a New Zealand-based researcher and a researcher at the host institution  
- Applications that seek to establish entirely new collaborations will not be considered  

## 6. Research Project

Each application must include a clearly defined research project to be undertaken during the visit.

The proposal should:

- Outline the research objectives and methodology  
- Demonstrate how the visit will contribute to the project’s success  
- Include a feasible timeline for completion  
- Identify expected research outputs  

The following conditions apply:

- Projects focused solely on the completion or writing up of a PhD thesis are not eligible  

Preference will be given to projects where:

- A primary goal is the preparation and submission of a peer-reviewed publication within a reasonable timeframe  
- The collaboration meaningfully advances the student’s PhD research  
- The work contributes to the broader New Zealand statistics and data science research community  

## 7. Application Requirements

Applicants must submit:

- A research proposal (maximum 2–3 pages)  
- A statement of purpose outlining the benefits of the visit  
- Evidence of the existing collaboration between supervisors/hosts  
- A brief CV of the student  
- Letters of support from:  
  - The student’s primary supervisor  
  - The host academic/researcher at the destination institution  

## 8. Selection Criteria

Applications will be assessed based on:

- Academic merit of the applicant  
- Quality and feasibility of the proposed research project  
- Strength and evidence of the existing collaboration  
- Likelihood of producing high-quality research outputs (e.g., publications)  
- Contribution to strengthening the New Zealand statistical and data science research ecosystem  

## 9. Administration

- The scholarship will be administered by a sub-committee of the NZSA Awards Committee  
- Members of this sub-committee will be appointed by the NZSA Awards Committee Convenor  
- The selection committee will comprise representatives from academia and, where appropriate, the wider statistical community  
- Reporting requirements may include a brief written report and/or presentation to the NZSA community upon completion of the visit  

## 10. Expected Outcomes

The scholarship is expected to:

- Foster wider and sustained research collaboration networks within and beyond New Zealand  
- Enhance the research experience and output of PhD students  
- Increase the rate of high-quality joint publications  
- Strengthen connections between universities, polytechnics, and public research organisations  
- Raise the profile of Statistics and Data Science within the New Zealand research landscape
